How to use these pages

Explore the five ways to build experience during your second year. At each station, open at least one CSUDH resource, notice what interests you, and choose one small next step.

No perfect choice is required. The goal is to explore possibilities—not to decide your entire future today.

💼 Station 2: Jobs & Internships

CSUDH HandshakeSearch for internships, part-time and full-time jobs, on-campus student assistant positions, work-study opportunities, career events, and workshops.
↗
Career CenterGet support with career exploration, resumes, interviews, internship searches, career fairs, graduate school, and professional development.

Career Coaching through TSCSchedule an appointment with a career coach through Toro Success Collaborative in your myCSUDH portal.

Digital Career ToolkitUse online tools for exploring majors and careers, preparing application materials, and building career-ready skills.

Your InstructorsAsk faculty in your field about projects, labs, creative work, programs, or next steps specific to your major. Use the directory when you need contact information.

Student Research ConferenceSee the research and creative work CSUDH students are doing—or work toward presenting your own project.

Research OpportunitiesExplore campus programs and external experiences that can help prepare you for careers or graduate school.

🤝 Station 4: Community Service & Volunteering

College CorpsA structured service program focused on early childhood education, K–12 education, food insecurity, and climate action. For 2026–27, eligible fellows may earn up to $10,000 after completing the program requirements.
↗
Toro LinkLook for service events and volunteer opportunities offered by student organizations and campus programs.
↗
Student OrganizationsMany clubs, fraternities, sororities, and major-based organizations include community-service projects.
↗
Service Connected to Your MajorExplore your department or degree program, then ask faculty about service-learning courses, field experiences, and community partners.
↗

💬 Station 5: Professional Relationships & Mentoring

Toro ConnectUse CSUDH’s online mentoring and networking platform to connect with Toro alumni by degree, industry, interests, and experiences.
↗
Faculty Office HoursAsk instructors about their field, research, graduate school, career paths, or ways students can get involved. Use the directory when you need contact information.
↗
Career CenterCareer coaches can help you identify people to learn from and prepare for networking conversations.
↗
Campus & Alumni EventsPanels, workshops, and industry events can provide low-pressure ways to meet professionals and hear how they got started.
